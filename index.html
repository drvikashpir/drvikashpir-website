This website is designed to help the public understand what Interventional Radiology can offer for different parts of the body. You can choose an organ or body system, then select one of three common medical problems:

Bleeding

IR can often help control internal bleeding by finding the bleeding blood vessel and blocking it using embolization techniques.

Blood Clot

IR may help selected patients with blood clots by using catheter-based treatments to remove clot, dissolve clot, improve blood flow, or place protective devices when needed.

Tumor

IR can help diagnose and treat selected tumors using image-guided biopsy, ablation, embolization, chemoembolization, or radioembolization.

What is Interventional Radiology?

Interventional Radiology is a medical specialty that uses imaging guidance such as ultrasound, CT scan, X-ray fluoroscopy, and MRI to perform precise minimally invasive procedures.

Instead of making large surgical cuts, an interventional radiologist often works through a tiny skin opening using needles, wires, catheters, balloons, stents, coils, particles, glue, heat, cold, or radiation-based therapies.

IR treatments are used across the body — including the brain, lungs, liver, kidneys, uterus, blood vessels, bones, spine, abdomen, and other organs.

When to Seek Emergency Help

If you or someone else has sudden severe bleeding, stroke symptoms, chest pain, severe shortness of breath, coughing large amounts of blood, sudden weakness, loss of consciousness, severe abdominal pain, or any life-threatening symptom, seek emergency medical care immediately.

Medical Disclaimer

This website is for public education only. It does not provide diagnosis, personal medical advice, treatment recommendation, or emergency medical care.

Interventional Radiology procedures may not be suitable for every patient. Treatment decisions should be made by qualified medical professionals after reviewing the patient’s symptoms, physical examination, imaging, medical history, laboratory results, and overall condition.

If you have a medical emergency, call emergency services immediately.
